Jon Hamm's Net Worth: Mad Men, Mad Money

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Sunday, July 14, 2024

Highlights

  • Jon Hamm's career went from minor roles to A-list superstar, grossing over $4.7 billion worldwide in 34 movies.
  • Hamm's breakthrough came as Don Draper on Mad Men, earning an Emmy and starring in high-grossing films like Top Gun: Maverick.
  • Jon Hamm's net worth, estimated at $45 million, has been boosted by high-paying endorsement deals, real estate investments, and lucrative acting roles.

Jon Hamm has been making movies for over twenty-eight years — his career is marked by critical acclaim, commercial success, and a transformative evolution from promising talent to an A-list superstar.

Breaking into the film industry with his debut in Clint Eastwood's Space Cowboys, Hamm initially garnered attention with more minor roles that set the stage for his monumental rise to fame.

Hamm came to the spotlight following his portrayal of Don Draper in the critically acclaimed AMC series Mad Men. The role earned him an Emmy and two Golden Globes.

It also propelled him into a series of high-profile film projects, culminating in approximately 34 films that have collectively grossed over $4.7 billion worldwide.

Hamm has delivered memorable performances that ranged from dramatic depth to comedic flair.

From his impeccably tailored suits to a bank account to match, Jon Hamm's net worth stands at an estimated $45 million.

Jon Hamm's Film And TV Career

After graduating from the University of Missouri with a Bachelor of Arts degree in English in 1993, Hamm initially pursued teaching before venturing into acting.

His relocation to Los Angeles in pursuit of his dreams was met with challenges, as he arrived with only $150 to his name, and faced numerous rejections at auditions.

However, Hamm's determination eventually paid off when he secured his first major television role on NBC's Providence in 2000, leading to subsequent opportunities in film and television.

In the early 2000s, Hamm established himself in Hollywood with roles in films such as Kissing Jessica Stein and We Were Soldiers.

The latter, a war film released in 2002, grossed over $115 million worldwide, contributing significantly to Hamm's rising prominence.

Hamm landed his breakthrough role in 2007 when he was chosen from over 80 candidates to play Don Draper, the protagonist of AMC's drama series Mad Men.

Mad Men debuted with almost 1.4 million viewers. It developed a loyal audience, with Hamm receiving strong reviews.

Hamm received his first Emmy for "Outstanding Lead Actor In A Drama Series," after receiving multiple nominations for acting in and producing the series.

While continuing his television success, Hamm's film career also flourished. He appeared in The Town, which was well-received and grossed $154 million worldwide.

In 2015, Jon Hamm landed a voice acting gig in Minions, playing Herb Overkill. Hamm also bagged a role in Edgar Wright's comedy crime film Baby Driver, which grossed $227 million.

This was followed by his involvement in Top Gun: Maverick, his highest-grossing film to date, which amassed over $1.5 billion worldwide.

Jon Hamm's Mad Men Salary

Jon Hamm's breakout role as Don Draper in the AMC period drama series Mad Men significantly boosted his financial standing.

Starting with a modest salary of $20,000 per episode in the first season, Hamm's compensation consistently increased as the show gained popularity.

By the second season, his per-episode rate was raised to $25,000, quadrupling to $100,000 in the third season.

Hamm's Mad Men salary continued to rise, reaching $125,000 per episode in season four, and ultimately peaking at $275,000 per episode in later seasons.

Apart from his earnings from Mad Men, Hamm's film and voice acting roles have also contributed to his earnings.

Although his salaries from most of his films are less frequently disclosed, his roles in blockbuster productions like Top Gun Maverick and Baby Driver – and his voice work in Minions, which grossed over $1 billion worldwide – likely earned him substantial fees, commensurate with his star status.

Jon Hamm's Real Estate Portfolio

Like many successful actors, Hamm has invested much of his wealth in property ownership.

In 2013, Hamm and his then-partner, Jennifer Westfeldt, bought a 1,000-square-foot penthouse in New York City's Upper West Side for $2.45 million.

Following their split, the penthouse underwent renovations, after which Hamm began leasing the property in 2017 for $15,000 per month.

The same year, Hamm expanded his property portfolio to the West Coast by acquiring a four-bedroom house in Los Feliz, Los Angeles, for $3.375 million.

This property adds a personal touch to his holdings, serving as a private residence rather than an investment property; while holdings are often undisclosed, property likely comprises a decent share of Jon Hamm's net worth.
