Early Life and Education of Sinbad

Sinbad, whose real name is David Adkins, was born on November 10, 1956, in Benton Harbor, Michigan. He grew up with five siblings and attended Benton Harbor High School. After graduating in 1974, he went on to study at the University of Denver, where he played basketball for the college team. However, a knee injury ended his athletic career.

During his time at the University of Denver, Sinbad discovered his comedic talents and began performing at local clubs and venues. His unique blend of storytelling and observational humor quickly gained him attention and popularity among audiences. It was during this time that he adopted the stage name “Sinbad,” inspired by the fictional sailor character.

Despite facing financial difficulties, Sinbad remained determined to pursue a career in entertainment. Sinbad’s ability to connect with audiences through his relatable humor and charismatic stage presence set him apart in the comedy scene.
